Journal Rankings

Ranking academic journals by elite institution authorship

Preview: Based on a partial sample (~5%) of the OpenAlex dataset. Rankings may shift with the full dataset. See methodology for details.

Methodology

Journals are ranked by the fraction of their papers that have at least one author from an elite ("tier") institution. The denominator includes only papers where OpenAlex identified at least one institutional affiliation.

Tier 1 (4 institutions): Harvard, Stanford, MIT, Oxford.
Tier 2 (23 institutions, inclusive of Tier 1): adds Google, Meta, Microsoft, NIH, US DHHS, Chinese Academy of Sciences, ETH Zurich, UCL, Imperial, U of Toronto, UC Berkeley, Princeton, Johns Hopkins, NUS, Cambridge, Tsinghua, U of Tokyo, Max Planck, CNRS.

Papers are filtered to type "article" with at least 10 cited references, excluding paratext. Data from OpenAlex (2015-2024). Full methodology.
